How To Make Strawberry Cake

Step By Step

Ingredients for Strawberry Cake

2, 1/2 Cups All-Purpose Flour
1, 3/4 Cups Granulated Sugar
2 tps Baking Powder
1/2 tps Salt
3/4 Cups Unsalted Butter

Cut The Butter Into Cubes.

5 Egg Whites
1/4 Cup Sour Cream
2tps Vanilla Extract
3/4 up Strawberry Reduction

How To Make A Strawberry Reduction

3/4 Cup ButterMilk
1-2 Drops Pink Gel Food Coloring is Optional

Strawberry Shortcut Swiss Meringue ButterCream

2/3 Cup Pasteurized Egg Whites
5 Cups Powdered Sugar
2 tsp Vanilla Extract
1/2 tsp SALT
2 Cups Unsalted Butter
1/2 Cup Strawberry Reduction

How To Make A Strawberry Reduction

Time To Do Strawberry Cake

1.

Preheat oven to 350 F.
Prepare Cake Pans With Nonstick Cooking Spray And Parchment Circle.
6-inch Pans
7-inch Pans
8-inch Pans
9-inch Pans

2.

In The Bowl Of A Stand Mixer Filled.
With A Paddle Attachment Or A Mixer Bowl.
With An Electric Hand Mixer, Mix Together.
All-Purpose Flour, Granulated Sugar, Baking Powder, Baking Soda,-
-And SALT On Low Speed Until Combined.

3.

Add Cubed Butter One Piece At A Time While Mixing on Low Speed.
Until All The Butter Is Added.
Continue To Mix Until The Mixture Resembles Wet Sand And Crumbles.

4.

Turn The Mixer Off.
Add In Egg Whites, Sour Cream, Vanilla Extract.
And Strawberry Reduction.
Turn The Mixer To Medium Speed.
And Mixer For One Mix For One Minute.

5.

Turn The Mixer To Low Speed And Slowly Drizzle In The ButterMilk.
Once All The ButterMilk Is Added, Scrape The Side And Button.
To Ensure All The Ingredients Are Well Incorporated.
Mix For An Additional 30 Second On Low Speed.

6.

Portion The Cake Batter Into The Prepared Cake Pans.
Use Kitchen Scale To Ensure The Layers Have Equal Amounts Of Batter.

7.

Bake The Cake layers For 25-30 Minutes.
The Cake Layers Are Done Baking.
When An Inserted Toothpick Comes Out Clean.
Or The Cake Bounces Back When Gently Poked.

8.

Remove The Pans From The oven.
Allow The Cake Layers To Cool In The Pans For 15 Minutes.
Before Removing The Cake.
And Transferring To A Wire Rack To Cool Completely.

Time To Do Strawberry Shortcut Swiss Meringue ButterCream

1.

In The Bowl Of Mixer Filled With A Whisk.
Attachment, Combine Pasteurized Liquid Egg Whites.
AND POWDERED SUGAR.
Mix On Low Speed Until Combined.
Scrape The Sides And Button Of The Button of The Bowl.
To Ensure The Ingredients Are Well Combined.

2.

Turen The Speed To medium-High And Whip The Egg White.
Mixer Until Thick And Opaque, Approximately 3 minutes.
(Note-The Egg White Mixture Will Not Whip Up To Stiff Peaks)

3.

Add In Salt And Vanilla Extract And Continue To Mix Until Combined.

4.

Turn The Mixer to Low Speed.
Add Butter, One TableSpoon At the Time.
Until All The Butter Is Added.
Allow The Butter To Mix For A Few Seconds In Between.
Each Addition Of Butter.

5.

Once The Butter Is Added, Turn The Mixer To Medium-High Speed.
And Whip The ButterCream For 5-10 Minutes.
The ButterCream May Appear To Curdle Or Separate-
-This Is Normal-Just Continue To Whip The Frosting.
Scrape The Sides And Bottom.
Of The Bowl Occasionally While Whipping.

6.

Once The ButterCream Appears Smooth And Creamy.
Add In The Strawberry Reduction And Mix On Medium Speed.
Until Well Combined Into The Frosting.
Scrape The Sides And Bottom Of The Bowl.
To Ensure Everything Is Well Combined.

7.

Once The ButterCream Is Smooth And Silky It Is Ready To Use.
If Desired, You Can Switch To The Paddle Attachment.
And Mix On The Lowest Speed For Several Minutes To Remove Air Bubbles.
And Make The ButterCream Very Smooth.

Assembling The Strawberry Cake

1.

Level The Cake Layers With A Serrated Knife.
Place One Cake Layer On A Cake Board Or Plate.
Add Desired Amount Of Frosting To The Top Of The Layer.
And Spread Evenly.
Continue To Repeat This Process Until The Layers Are Stacked.

2.

Spread A Very Thin Layer Of Frosting All Over The Cake (Crumb Coat).
Chill Your Cake For 30 Minutes.
Until The ButterCream Frosting Is Slightly Firm To The Touch.
